Project Pixer

The challenge

People sometimes feel thrown into the wolves den while searching for a person that could match them. They can feel it's frightening to put themselves out there and feel rejection or it could be that they don't feel they can find "the one" because of they are hiding behind a hidden transaction wall or a membership fee. Never the less there are hurdles they need to overcome.

So, How might we overcome these hurdles of transaction walls?

How might we give people a chance to get to know eachother?

How might we let people not feel like it is all about the looks?

Removing barriers to usability

Taking a holistic approach rather than focusing on one issue at a time, I figured that the main issue here is most likely that there are many people/organizations that pray on lonely and desperate people. There needs to be some way to make every user equaly acountable for their actions and reactions toward other users.

In the swedish market there is a widely used authenticator called BankID that uses social security numbers to verify the person who signs in to a service. If this could be implemented the users would not swerve out in mean comments, and if there are other things going on this would be reported and connected to that specific person.

Core function

The first thing wee need to implement is the way we sign in and create the user-profile. A few simple steps with "this-or-that" questions followed by an optional comment about why that choise was made. This will have a great impact while scrolling through the feed of several users.

In this application there will be no images of the users shown at first. Just four pictures representing the choisess we made earlier, this to keep it simple and fast to scroll. When the user finds someone they simply start chatting, then they might find themselves to the point where they would like to meet. Then they book a date through the app and 12 hours before the date takes place they each recieve a picture of whom they will be meeting. And from the point they set the date both are able to reschedule och cancel the date.

By letting companys advertice in the app we remove the need for expensive memberships. It's not as clean but I believe at this point that segregation comes at a higher cost.

Validation and outcome

The color-scheme gives a light and friendly feel to the user and keeps it trustworthy. The comments tests have given to the design have been mostly positive while some are not too fond on the advertisement. But while asked about the alternative as in memberships, they all aggreed with me. This tells me that we are on the right track but could bennefit with more extensive testing.

The first iteration of account-registration was designed as most competitors do, questions about interests and this kind but this often falls in the background adn tend to be less important. But with removing the image at the start lets this become more relevant and makes it so that users get to know eachother more deeply before the first date. And by making it EAVEN easier with a visual representation of the coise and the optional comment it was one of the positive thing my testing noted.

The last thing that the tests showed was to help the user even more by implementing some sort of giud or vidoe to help understand why or what they were actually doing instead of hoping that the users would like to experiment with it. And as a sidenote we maybe could make the events locally and perhaps even more personal. Who knows we might even be able to put in some soet of advertising further there.
